## FINDINGS

### A. Container External Condition
The exterior of reefer container HLBU 950 992 4 was observed to be clean and in good external condition. The reefer unit, identified as HLHG 820200, exhibited general dirt and grime; however, no obvious major structural damage was noted. Security seals AMERICOLD 0004004 and UL-5348587 were intact and properly secured on the container door locking bars. The CSC Safety Approval plate indicated the container type as MQRS-40HS-062A, manufactured in 10/2020 by Maersk Container Industry Qingdao Ltd., and operated by Hapag-Lloyd Germany, with a next examination date of ACEP-D HH-3.

### B. Reefer Unit Performance
The reefer unit digital display indicated a SETPOINT of -17.7°C. A RETURN air temperature of -13.5°C and a SUPPLY air temperature of -17.8°C were observed. A differential of 4.2°C was noted between the RETURN air temperature and the SETPOINT/SUPPLY air temperature. This differential suggests a high heat load or compromised airflow, which can result in elevated cargo temperatures despite the "IN-RANGE" indicator being illuminated.

### C. Cargo Stowage and Packaging
Cargo boxes branded 'Hatfield', containing "PORK TONGUE TRIM" (Net Weight: 15.0 kg / 33.07 lb, Status: KEEP FROZEN, PKG ON 11/11/25, USDA approved for export), were observed. One cardboard cargo box exhibited a crushed or dented corner. Some cargo labels appeared slightly crumpled. The cargo was stacked high within the container, which may have impeded return air circulation. The top right stack of boxes was noted to be slightly displaced.

### D. Cargo Condition
Frozen meat in blue plastic bags exhibited significant frost and ice accumulation on the surface. Surface temperature readings of the frozen meat ranged from -7.7°C to -10.0°C. These temperatures are above typical deep freeze requirements. Visible dark reddish liquid/blood was present on the surface of some frozen meat. Evidence of thawing and refreezing was observed, including irregular ice crystal formation and a partially defrosted appearance with congealed blood/liquid on meat surfaces. Frosting indicates that the product has thawed and then partially refrozen. Blood serum separation can occur if the temperature of chilled meat falls below -1.4 °C, leading to muscle cell rupture and increased susceptibility to microbiological deterioration. Reddish discoloration, liquid, and pooled stains, identified as blood, were present on the T-bar floor of the container. Surface corrosion and rust were also noted on the floor. A USDA rejection tag was observed with "U.S. REJECTED" checked, citing "Thawed / Re froze / Bad smell" for the item "TRAILER - PIG TONGUE" (Serial Number: B 30 333802). A bad smell indicates microbiological spoilage, which is directly linked to temperature abuse.

### E. Temperature Monitoring
A TempTale.Ultra SENSI TECH temperature data logger displayed a temperature of 12.0 °F (approx. -11.1°C). The data logger was configured for a temperature range of 22°F to 15°F (approx. -5.5°C to -9.4°C). This programmed range is above the optimal temperature for frozen goods (typically 0°F / -18°C). Another TempTale.Ultra SENSI TECH data logger had a blank or unreadable screen.

## RECOMMENDATIONS FOR MITIGATION OF LOSS
The cargo should be thoroughly inspected at discharge to identify all affected cartons. Detailed temperature records from the reefer unit and any functional data loggers should be retrieved and analyzed to establish a complete temperature profile of the voyage. Cargo exhibiting signs of thawing, refreezing, or spoilage should be segregated. Improved stowage practices are recommended to ensure adequate airflow around and through the cargo to prevent temperature hotspots.

---

## EXTENT OF LOSS / RECOMMENDED LOSS RESERVE
Quantification of loss is pending full cargo inspection and detailed temperature data analysis. Given the USDA rejection and visible signs of spoilage, a significant portion of the consignment is likely compromised.

## ADDITIONAL COMMENTS
The presence of blood and frost on the cargo and container floor is indicative of temperature abuse, specifically thawing and refreezing. The discrepancy between the reefer unit set point and the data logger's programmed range, coupled with elevated surface temperatures of the product, suggests that the cargo was exposed to temperatures inconsistent with "KEEP FROZEN" requirements. The 'bad smell' noted on the USDA rejection tag is a strong indicator of microbiological deterioration.
