SURVEYOR'S FINDINGS:
A Hapag-Lloyd 45R1 reefer container, ID HLBU 950 992 4, was inspected. The container’s external condition exhibited general dirt and grime, with no significant structural damage observed on the side panels or overall. The reefer unit, model/ID HLHG 820700 1, appeared operational with visible fans and control panel, showing general wear and tear but no obvious heavy damage. Warning labels were present and legible on the unit. The CSC Safety Approval Plate was present and legible, indicating manufacturing in 10/2020 by MAERSK CONTAINER INDUSTRY QINGDAO LTD..

The reefer unit display indicated a setpoint of -17.7°C, a return air temperature of -13.5°C, and a supply air temperature of -17.8°C. The return air temperature was noted to be significantly warmer than both the setpoint and the supply air temperature.

Security seals were intact. A yellow plastic bolt seal, number UL-5348587, was observed on a container door locking bar. A second yellow plastic bolt seal, number 0004004, branded "AMERICOLD", was also observed intact on a container door locking bar.

The interior of the loaded container revealed a clean and well-maintained T-bar floor. Cargo cartons, branded "Hatfield" and identified as "PORK TONGUE TRIM," were stacked evenly and appeared to be within load lines, filling the container. Product labels indicated "FOR EXPORT TO THE PHILIPPINES" and included a USDA inspection stamp. The cartons generally appeared intact, with some minor wear observed; however, one carton was noted with a crushed and torn corner, compromising the packaging integrity.

Reddish liquid, identified as purge/myoglobin, was observed in the channels of the T-bar floor. Significant accumulation of this liquid was noted, indicative of substantial leakage from the cargo. Rust spots were visible on the T-bar floor mixed with the liquid. Organic debris, appearing to be small pieces of cardboard/packaging, was present within the liquid in the floor channels. This condition indicates compromised hygiene and potential for corrosion of the aluminum floor.

Temperature readings from a TempTale.Ultra data logger, model TUA00.01.62B, indicated a temperature of 12.0°F (-11.1°C). Multiple readings of the frozen pork tongue trim using an infrared thermometer indicated product temperatures ranging from -07.5°C to -10.0°C. These temperatures consistently exceeded the optimal storage temperature of 0°F (-18°C) for frozen meat.

The frozen pork tongue trim, individually plastic-wrapped within cardboard cartons, exhibited visible frost and large ice crystals. Reddish liquid (purge/myoglobin) was present at the bottom of the plastic bags, with significant amounts collected in some bags, indicating substantial thawing and leakage. Some product pieces showed signs of discoloration. The presence of frosting, large ice crystals, and purge is indicative of temperature abuse and repeated freeze-thaw cycles, leading to muscle cell rupture and blood serum separation.

An official U.S. DEPARTMENT OF AGRICULTURE FOOD SAFETY AND INSPECTION SERVICE "U.S. REJECTED" tag, No. B 30 333802, was observed. The tag specified the item as "TRAILER - Pig Tongue" and listed the reasons for tagging as "Thawed / Leaky / Bad smell". The date of tag application was 12/20/24.

NATURE/CAUSE OF LOSS:
The cargo sustained temperature abuse, evidenced by elevated product temperatures, significant blood serum separation (purge), and extensive frost and ice crystal formation. These conditions are consistent with thawing and refreezing cycles during transit, which causes muscle cell rupture and degradation of product quality. The presence of purge on the T-bar floor indicates product leakage, leading to unsanitary conditions. The product was formally rejected by the USDA due to being thawed, leaky, and having a bad smell, which are direct consequences of temperature abuse.

PACKING:
Merchandise was packaged in Hatfield branded cardboard cartons containing individually plastic-wrapped frozen pork tongue trim. One carton was observed to have compromised packaging integrity.
